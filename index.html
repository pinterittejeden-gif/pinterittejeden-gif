Research discipline

A proposal should reveal where it can fail

Scientific language is useful only when it makes uncertainty easier to inspect. Every project follows the same four-layer structure.

  1. 01

    Established basis

    Published mechanisms and existing practices that support individual components.

  2. 02

    Proposed bridge

    The original engineering inference connecting those components into a system.

  3. 03

    Critical unknowns

    Biological, ecological, and material assumptions most likely to invalidate the idea.

  4. 04

    Minimum proof

    Controls, measurements, and failure thresholds required before stronger claims.

About

Built to learn in public

I'm Pinteritte, an independent student exploring synthetic biology, biomaterials, environmental biotechnology, and ecological restoration through conceptual system design.

The goal is not to make finished-research claims. It is to practice asking better questions: What mechanism would carry the design? What would selection pressure break? What measurement would change my mind?
