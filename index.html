the serious bit

I like weird ideas. I don't like pretending they're proven.

So every project is split into four parts. This stops the fun idea and the actual evidence from quietly becoming the same thing.

  1. 01

    what I found

    The published mechanisms and existing work I could actually find support for.

  2. 02

    my leap

    The bit where I connect those mechanisms into something that does not exist yet.

  3. 03

    what could kill it

    The biological, ecological, or material problems most likely to wreck the idea.

  4. 04

    what I'd test first

    The smallest useful experiment that could tell me whether to continue or give up.

about me (short version)

I'm Pinteritte. Biology gives me too many ideas.

This site is where I put the ones I can't stop thinking about. I especially like synthetic biology, biomaterials, environmental biotech, and ecology—basically anything where living systems run into engineering problems.

I know these ideas can be wrong. That's part of why they're public. If you find a broken assumption, a paper I missed, or a much simpler answer, I genuinely want to know.
